I teach sociology at the University of Arizona.

My research interests are in economic sociology, the sociology of culture, the sociology of organizations, and social theory. I have written a book about the social organization of exchange in human blood and organs. I’ve also published work on the relationship between information technology and cultural policy, wage growth and unemployment in OECD labor markets, and debates about structure and agency in social theory. Right now I am interested in the moral order of market society, the effect of quantification on the emergence and stabilization of social categories, and the link between these two topics.
